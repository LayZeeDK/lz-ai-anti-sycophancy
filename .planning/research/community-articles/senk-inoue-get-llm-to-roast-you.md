## Summary

Provides actionable "context engineering" strategies for overcoming LLM sycophancy, particularly in educational settings. Argues that the problems with LLMs are really human problems (cognitive bias) and that effective anti-sycophancy requires providing detailed persona instructions rather than simple requests for criticism.

## Why LLMs Are Overly Agreeable

"Companies design them to be pleasant, and human feedback training reinforces agreeable responses. The problems we encounter with LLMs are really human problems -- in this case, cognitive bias."

## Key Strategy: Context Engineering

Rather than requesting "constructive criticism," provide detailed persona instructions that establish:
- **Motivation** -- why the persona cares about being critical
- **Perspective** -- what rhetorical posture to inhabit
- **Tone** -- what level of directness is authorized
- **Emotional stakes** -- what situation to respond to

## Practical Persona Examples

1. **Michiko Kakutani-style critic:** Sharp, culturally literate analysis
2. **Embittered academic turned standup comic:** Roasting embedded in power structures
3. **Don Draper meets Churchill:** Gravitas-laden strategic work
4. **Reviewer Number 2:** Harsh academic peer review simulation
5. **Jaded Gen Z student:** Evaluating course content authenticity

## Critical Implementation Detail

Personas set *tone* but do not replace targeted follow-up questions. Pair character assignment with specific prompts like "Where are we losing the thread?" for maximum effectiveness.

## Relevance to Our Project

MEDIUM -- The persona-based approach is interesting but targeted at individual prompts rather than system-level instructions. However, the insight that "context engineering" (filling the context window with interpretive rules) is more effective than simple directives is directly applicable to CLAUDE.md / AGENTS.md design. The distinction between tone-setting and targeted questioning is useful.
