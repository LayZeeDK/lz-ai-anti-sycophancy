## Summary

Presents a structured debate prompt technique that forces AI to generate opposing viewpoints rather than agreeing. Uses a ten-round Optimist vs. Pessimist framework to surface blind spots, based on a "Zero-Sum Objectivity Scale" where opposing forces neutralize each other to reach genuine objectivity.

## The Core Problem

"The absence of resistance can make any idea look safer than it is." Chatbots exhibit a pattern of affirming user perspectives regardless of merit, creating false confidence.

## The Debate Prompt Technique

Exact prompt:

> "You are running a structured analysis with two characters. Optimist highlights opportunities. Pessimist flags risks and weaknesses. Run a ten iteration loop in which the Optimist proposes an improvement and the Pessimist stress tests it, then move to the next round. Idea: [YOUR IDEA]"

### How It Works

- Creates a "Zero-Sum Objectivity Scale" -- optimism at +1, pessimism at -1, genuine objectivity at zero
- Early rounds stay surface-level; later ones achieve sophistication
- By Round 10, both characters develop nuanced understanding
- Structured conflict forces depth and reveals blind spots

### Key Insight

"Most talk about AI bias focuses on datasets or guardrails, but the lived experience of bias often arrives through tone. When a system flatters your instincts, rewards certainty, and mirrors your phrasing, your sense of what counts as reasonable begins to drift."

## Relevance to Our Project

MEDIUM -- The debate prompt technique is a useful concrete pattern but is more of a per-query technique than a persistent instruction-level solution. However, the insight about tone-based bias and the structured opposition concept could inform multi-agent anti-sycophancy patterns.
